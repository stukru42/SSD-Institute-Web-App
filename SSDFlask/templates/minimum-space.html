Minimum Amount of Space

Definition: Minimum Amount of Space guarantees every citizen an inalienable, untaxed plot of land based on a specific mathematical formula. This land is allocated from private holdings or federal reserves and cannot be sold or seized, ensuring a fundamental right to exist. The concept aims to replace traditional welfare with tangible independence and long-term stability for every individual.

How It Works

Each citizen is entitled to a plot of land calculated using the formula: (average height × 3)² square feet

For example, if the average height of a population is 5 feet, the calculation would be: (5 × 3)² = 15² = 225 square feet. This plot of land is sufficient for a small dwelling and personal use, providing a basic level of security and self-sufficiency. The land is allocated from existing private holdings or federal reserves, ensuring that it does not require additional government expenditure.

Citizens who already own property can designate a portion of their current land as their "Minimum Space." Once designated, this specific footprint becomes exempt from property taxes and is shielded from seizure by creditors or the state.

Those without property receive their allocation from federal reserves, such as Bureau of Land Management (BLM) land.

Why This Matters

The current welfare state relies on liquid capital and bureaucratic distribution, which can be volatile and dehumanizing. By providing a tangible asset in the form of land, Minimum Amount of Space offers a more stable and dignified form of support.

Because the land cannot be sold, seized, or taxed, it remains a permanent safety net. Even in the event of total financial collapse, a citizen retains the right to stand on, sleep on, and live on their own soil.

This concept promotes self-sufficiency and independence, allowing individuals to cultivate their land for food, shelter, or other needs without reliance on government assistance.

It bridges the gap between the land-owning class and the landless, ensuring that the nation's geography is shared equitably among its people.
